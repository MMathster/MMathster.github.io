## About

I am an independent mathematician and analyst whose work centers on extending classical mathematical problems into generalized and non-standard domains. My approach combines symbolic rigor, structural pattern recognition, and computational verification.

Rather than treating pure and applied mathematics as separate disciplines, I focus on the continuity between them: formal derivation, structural generalization, and algorithmic validation.

---

## Research & Technical Focus

### Higher Analysis & Special Functions

My recent work concentrates on advanced integral evaluation and structural generalization of special functions.

- Development of identities involving Meijer G-functions and generalized hypergeometric functions ($_{p}F_{q}$)
- Closed-form analysis of polylogarithmic and multiple-zeta integrals beyond standard reference tables
- Structural treatment of integrals involving floor, fractional-part, and nearest-integer functions
- Logical partitioning techniques for non-smooth analytical domains

This line of work emphasizes abstraction, functional unification, and rigorous generalization.

---

### Geometry & Structural Pattern Analysis

My earlier research explored classical geometry through modern analytical methods.

- Hybrid analytic and Euclidean approaches to Sangaku-style problems
- Investigation of triangle center loci and structural invariants
- Derivation of geometric signatures in constrained configurations

These projects established my long-term interest in pattern extraction and invariant structure across mathematical systems.

---

### Computational & Analytical Integration

My experience as a Programmer Analyst strengthened the implementation layer of my mathematical work.

- Workflow automation and data structuring using Python and SQL
- Numerical validation of symbolic results
- Cross-verification between theoretical derivation and computational output

This integration allows me to move fluidly between abstract reasoning and executable models.
